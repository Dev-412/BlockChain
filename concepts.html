Web3 Concepts

Core blockchain fundamentals explained side-by-side. Clear, concise, and visual — no textbook jargon.

🌐 Web2 vs Web3

VS

🏢 Web2

  • Companies own your data (Google, Meta)
  • Centralized servers — single point of failure
  • Login with email & password
  • Platform can ban or censor you
  • Revenue flows to corporations

🔗 Web3

  • You own your data and digital assets
  • Decentralized network — no single owner
  • Login with your wallet (MetaMask)
  • Censorship-resistant by design
  • Revenue flows to users & creators

⟠ Ethereum vs Bitcoin

VS

₿ Bitcoin

  • Digital gold — a store of value
  • Limited scripting, no smart contracts
  • 21 million max supply (deflationary)
  • Proof of Work consensus (mining)
  • Focused on being sound money

⟠ Ethereum

  • World computer — programmable blockchain
  • Full smart contract support (Solidity)
  • No hard cap, but EIP-1559 burn mechanism
  • Proof of Stake consensus (staking)
  • Powers DeFi, NFTs, DAOs, and Layer 2s

🔑 Public Key vs Private Key

VS

📬 Public Key

  • Like your home address — safe to share
  • Used to receive crypto and verify signatures
  • Derived from your private key mathematically
  • Anyone can see your balance on-chain
  • Also called your wallet address

🔐 Private Key

  • Like your house key — never share it
  • Used to sign transactions and prove ownership
  • Losing it means losing all your assets forever
  • Stored in your wallet (MetaMask, Ledger)
  • "Not your keys, not your crypto"

🗄️ Blockchain vs Traditional DB

VS

🗃️ Traditional DB

  • Controlled by a single admin or company
  • Data can be edited, deleted, or hidden
  • Fast reads/writes — optimized for speed
  • Trust the operator to be honest
  • Private by default (access-controlled)

⛓️ Blockchain

  • Distributed across thousands of nodes
  • Data is immutable — once written, forever
  • Slower, but cryptographically verifiable
  • Trustless — verify, don't trust
  • Transparent — anyone can audit the data
